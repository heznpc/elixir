## Items marked REVISE (≥6/9 flags) — per-criterion model reasons

### GHI-01 — Consumable hoarding (rational saving) (D2)
> "I save powerful consumable items in my game inventory for a moment of need that rarely comes."

- **SCOPE** (3/3 flag):
  - haiku: 🚩 violates: "Saving consumables is near-universal in engaged play; the qualifier 'rarely comes' lacks explicit distress, impairment, or loss-of-control markers."
  - sonnet: 🚩 violates: "The stem describes a near-universal saving behavior with no distress, impairment, frequency escalation, or loss-of-control marker."
  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Saving powerful consumables for a rarely-arriving moment of need is near-universal among engaged players, with no frequency, distress, or loss-of-control marker."

- **OVERPATHOLOGIZING** (3/3 flag):
  - haiku: 🚩 violates: "Outcome marker alone insufficient to distinguish rational saving from pathological hoarding without explicit loss of control or escalation."
  - sonnet: 🚩 violates: "Saving items for future need is normatively rational under incomplete information; the stem frames this adaptive behavior as inherently symptom-like."
  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Frames normatively rational saving of consumables for uncertain future challenges as problematic, without any distress, impairment, or loss-of-control marker."

### GHI-03 — Gacha pursuit (set completion) (D3)
> "I have spent money on randomised in-game purchases (loot boxes, gacha pulls) primarily to complete a set or roster rather than to obtain a single specific item."

- **SCOPE** (3/3 flag):
  - haiku: 🚩 violates: "Describes normal gacha play (spending to complete sets) without markers of frequency, escalation, distress, or loss-of-control distinguishing it from healthy engagement."
  - sonnet: 🚩 violates: "Spending on gacha for set completion is common among engaged players with no frequency, distress, or loss-of-control marker included."
  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Stem describes set-completion gacha spending without any frequency, distress, impairment, or loss-of-control marker distinguishing it from normal engaged player behavior."

- **OVERPATHOLOGIZING** (3/3 flag):
  - haiku: 🚩 violates: "Describes set completion (normatively rational, designed behavior) without markers of excess, framing normal engagement as potential symptom."
  - sonnet: 🚩 violates: "Set completion is a normatively rational, designer-intended goal; the item frames this ordinary motivation as inherently symptomatic without any distress or impairment marker."
  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Stem frames set-completion gacha purchasing as symptomatic without any distress, impairment, escalation, or loss-of-control marker, pathologizing a designed collection mechanic."

### GHI-15 — Backlog accumulation (sale purchasing) (D6)
> "During digital-storefront sales I have bought games I knew I would probably not play, because I felt I might want them later."

- **SCOPE** (3/3 flag):
  - haiku: 🚩 violates: "Sale purchasing without planned play is ubiquitous among typical gamers; no markers of distress, frequency escalation, or loss of control."
  - sonnet: 🚩 violates: "Buying discounted games speculatively is near-universal engaged-player behavior with no distress, impairment, or loss-of-control marker."
  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Buying sale games one may not play is essentially universal among engaged players; stem lacks frequency, distress, or loss-of-control markers."

- **OVERPATHOLOGIZING** (3/3 flag):
  - haiku: 🚩 violates: "Frames normatively rational sale-purchasing as pathological without distress, impairment, or loss-of-control markers."
  - sonnet: 🚩 violates: "Buying on sale for possible future use is normatively rational price-hedging behavior, yet the item frames it as inherently symptomatic."
  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Buying discounted games for possible future play is normatively rational sale behavior; stem lacks distress, impairment, or loss-of-control markers."

### GHI-18 — Emotional attachment (virtual sentimental value) (Cross)

- **SCOPE** (3/3 flag):

  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Keeping sentimentally valued but functionally useless items is near-universal among engaged players, with no frequency, distress, or loss-of-control marker."

- **OVERPATHOLOGIZING** (3/3 flag):

  - sonnet: 🚩 violates: "Sentimental attachment to mementos is normatively rational behavior framed implicitly as a symptom with no distress or impairment marker."
  - opus: 🚩 violates: "Keeping sentimentally meaningful items is a normatively rational, universal human behavior; framing it as a symptom pathologizes ordinary attachment."
